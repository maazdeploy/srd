What SRD actually means

SRD stands for Social Relief of Distress — a monthly grant from SASSA aimed at people with little or no income, currently paid out as the R370 grant. Once you've applied, "checking your SRD status" simply means asking SASSA's system whether that month's application was approved, is still pending, or was declined. For the full site, visit SRD Status Check.

Four ways to check

Official website

srd.sassa.gov.za — enter your ID number and the phone number registered on your application.

WhatsApp

Message SASSA's official WhatsApp number and follow the prompts from the automated assistant.

USSD code

Dial the current SASSA status code from your registered phone — no data needed.

Call centre

Phone the toll-free SASSA line on weekdays if your online check won't load or looks wrong.

From application to payment

1. You apply or you're already active

Your SRD application stays on file — SASSA re-checks it every month rather than asking you to reapply.

2. Monthly re-verification

Your ID is checked against Home Affairs, SARS and bank records to confirm you still qualify.

3. Status is set

Approved, pending, or declined — visible through any of the four channels above.

4. Payment (if approved)

A payment date is issued and funds are released through your chosen payment method. For the broader SASSA picture, see SASSA Status Check.

Questions people ask

What is the SRD grant?

A SASSA payment for South Africans with little or no other income, currently disbursed as the R370 grant.

Where do I check my SRD status?

On the official SRD portal with your ID and registered phone number, or via WhatsApp, USSD, or the call centre.

Why is my status still pending after weeks?

It usually means a details mismatch during the monthly cross-check — calling the SASSA line can confirm what's registered against your ID.

Do I need to reapply every month?

No. SASSA re-assesses your existing application automatically; you just check the result.
